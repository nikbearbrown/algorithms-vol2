# Figure brief — Chapter 04: Game Theory

## Recommended figures

### Figure 1 — Prisoner's Dilemma payoff matrix
**Path:** `../images/chapter-4-prisoners-dilemma.jpg`
**Description:** Standard 2×2 payoff matrix with strategies (Cooperate, Defect) on rows and columns. Cells labeled with (T, S) / (R, R) / (P, P) / (S, T). Nash equilibrium (D, D) highlighted; Pareto optimum (C, C) circled separately. Caption: "Rational play picks the Pareto-dominated outcome."
**Use:** referenced in §5 (Classical games).

### Figure 2 — Best-response dynamics convergence
**Path:** `../images/chapter-4-best-response-dynamics.jpg`
**Description:** Two-player game with strategy space visualized in 2D. Trajectory of best-response updates shown as arrows converging to a Nash equilibrium for one game; cycling in another (showing convergence is not guaranteed).
**Use:** referenced in §4 (Nash equilibrium and best response).

### Figure 3 — Auction format equivalences
**Path:** `../images/chapter-4-auction-formats.jpg`
**Description:** Four-quadrant diagram. Rows: Sealed-bid vs Open. Columns: First-price vs Second-price equivalent. Cells: First-price sealed-bid, Second-price (Vickrey), Dutch (descending), English (ascending). Strategic-equivalence arrows between cells. Caption: "Four formats, two strategic equivalence classes."
**Use:** referenced in §6 (Information and Bayesian games).

### Figure 4 — Three perspectives on platform pricing
**Path:** `../images/chapter-4-platform-pricing-perspectives.jpg`
**Description:** Three side-by-side game diagrams. Left: Platform vs aggregated users (one-sided optimization). Middle: Platform vs competitor platform (Bertrand duopoly). Right: Drivers as strategic agents in coordination game. Each panel annotated with the equilibrium concept that applies.
**Use:** referenced in §8 (Worked example).

### Figure 5 — Ultimatum Game empirical vs theoretical
**Path:** `../images/chapter-4-ultimatum-game.jpg`
**Description:** Bar chart. X-axis: offer percentage. Y-axis: frequency. Two bars per offer level: theoretical Nash prediction (almost all offers near zero, all accepted) vs empirical distribution (most offers near 50%, rejection threshold around 25-30%). Caption: "Where the rational-agent model fails."
**Use:** referenced in §10 (Failure modes).
