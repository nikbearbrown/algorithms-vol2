# Figure brief — Chapter 06: Stable Matching and Gale-Shapley

## Recommended figures

### Figure 1 — Stable vs unstable matching
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-stable-vs-unstable.jpg`
**Description:** Same set of applicants and programs with two different matchings shown side by side. Left: unstable — a blocking pair (m, w) highlighted where both prefer each other to current matches. Right: stable — no blocking pair exists. Arrows show preference rankings.
**Use:** referenced in §3 (Stable matching problem).

### Figure 2 — Gale-Shapley round-by-round
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-gale-shapley-rounds.jpg`
**Description:** Four-panel sequence of GS algorithm rounds on a small example (4 applicants, 4 programs). Each panel shows current proposals (arrows from m to w), tentative acceptances (solid edges), rejections (dashed edges with X). Final panel: stable matching.
**Use:** referenced in §4 (Gale-Shapley algorithm).

### Figure 3 — Lattice of stable matchings
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-stable-matching-lattice.jpg`
**Description:** Hasse diagram of the lattice of stable matchings for a small example. Top: proposer-optimal (m-side best, w-side worst). Bottom: receiver-optimal (m-side worst, w-side best). Intermediate matchings ordered by who-prefers-which. Caption: "Choice of proposer determines extreme; intermediate stable matchings exist."
**Use:** referenced in §4 (Proposer-optimal stability) and §8 (Failure modes).

### Figure 4 — NRMP scale and history
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-nrmp-history.jpg`
**Description:** Timeline showing NRMP milestones (1952 creation, decades of program-proposing, 1998 redesign to applicant-proposing). Bar chart showing year-over-year participants. Annotation on 1998 redesign with rationale.
**Use:** referenced in §7 (Worked example).

### Figure 5 — Strategy-proofness asymmetry
**Path:** `../images/chapter-6-strategy-proofness.jpg`
**Description:** Two-panel comparison. Left: applicant-proposing GS — applicants tell truth as dominant strategy. Right: same algorithm — programs may benefit by truncating preference list. Caption: "Stability + strategy-proofness on both sides is impossible (Roth 1982)."
**Use:** referenced in §5 (Strategy-proofness).
